## ARGUMENT FOR THE ARB

### The Legal Standard

**Tex. Tax Code §41.43(b)(3):**
> "The appraisal of the property is unequal … [if it] exceeds the median appraised value of a reasonable number of comparable properties appropriately adjusted."

### Your Argument (Informal Meeting)

*"I'm requesting a reduction from $836,185 to $808,789 based on unequal appraisal.*

*Your evidence shows three issues:*

1. *Your market sales indicate $248.49/sqft, but I'm appraised at $250.06/sqft — I'm overvalued by market standards.*

2. *Your equity comps (mostly Rolling Thunder Rd) indicated $852,928, but you valued me at $836,185 — you already discounted $17K from your own analysis.*

3. *But the strongest argument: Three homes on my own street — Angel Falls Dr — are far more comparable to mine than Rolling Thunder Rd homes. Same street, same VB2 class, same age (2015–2016), same land value ($183,425), within 5% of my square footage.*

*Those three homes are appraised at $241–242 per sqft. Applying that median ($241.87/sqft) to my 3,344 sqft gives $808,789. That's the value I'm requesting.*

*This is defensible under §41.43(b)(3) as a reasonable number of comparable properties, appropriately adjusted. In fact, it's more conservative than your own market and equity analyses suggest."*
