DO'S AND DON'Ts
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DO:
  ✓ Lead with street-level comps (strongest argument)
  ✓ Be polite and professional
  ✓ Stick to data (let numbers speak)
  ✓ Cite the statute by name (§41.43(b)(3))
  ✓ Use DCAD's own evidence against them
  ✓ Accept a reasonable settlement ($815K–$825K)
  ✓ Stay calm if they counter

DON'T:
  ✗ Mention what you paid for the home (can hurt your case in TX)
  ✗ Say "my taxes are too high" (only value is in scope)
  ✗ Accept lowball first offer without countering
  ✗ Get emotional or argumentative
  ✗ Reference unrelated properties or circumstances
  ✗ Commit to accepting offer without writing it down

  → "For market value, yes. But for §41.43(b)(3) equity analysis, the statute

     These three are far more comparable than homes a few blocks away on a
     different street."
